Textbook Resources

Some textbook resources I found helpful, categorized roughly by topics and difficulty.

Physics

Electromagnetism

  • Zangwill Andrew (1988). Modern Electrodynamics
  • Jackson J. D. (1999) Classical Electrodynamics (Classic textbook on electrodynamics)
  • Smythe William (1989) Static and Dynamic Electricity (This book is on the difficult side of the spectrum for undergraduate reading, but the author shows off his math skills so much that it’s cool to read.)

Quantum Mechanics

  • Sakurai, J. J.; Napolitano, Jim (2020). Modern Quantum Mechanics
  • Eisberg, Robert; Resnick, Robert (1985). Quantum Physics of Atoms, Molecules, Solids, Nuclei, and Particles 2e (Main textbook used by CKQuantumBros. If you’re interested, you can go to the YouTube channel!)

Quantum Field Theory

  • Lancaster, Tom; Blundell, Stephen, J. (2014). Quantum Field Theory for the Gifted Amateur (This is the textbook used in Professor’s Hsieh’s Quantum Field Theory I course)
  • Peskin, M. E.; Schroeder, D. Y. (2018). An Introduction to Quantum Field Theory(A much more rigorous book. But like many classics, this book has some very important steps either skipped or put in a strange logical order, which makes having a lot of discussions online a very fortunate thing.)

Biophysics

  • David Tong: Lectures on Mathematical Biology
  • Nelson, Philip (2001). Biological Physics Energy, Information, Life

Algebra

  • Dummit D. S.; Foote, R. M. (2003). Abstract Algebra 3e

(2024 Fall) Density Functional Theory

2024 Fall Density Functional Theory (Prof. Chai Jeng-Da)

Density functional theory (DFT) was developed by Walter Kohn, (1998 Nobel Prize in
Chemistry laureate), and Pierre Hohenberg to study the ground-state (GS) properties of large
systems using first principle methods. DFT can be used to study electronic systems, including
atoms, molecules, and solids…

Taking the course as a sophomore proved to be a huge challenge, but it was very worth it! At the end, I came back with an A+ and a whole lot of knowledge on solid state physics calculations.

I update my notes from time to time.

See my notes here

For my final report, I presented the paper Dion, M. et al. (2004). Van der Waals density functional for general geometries. You can see the paper here. If you’d like to see my presentation, feel free to contact me!
