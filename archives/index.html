Textbook Resources

Here are some textbook resources I found very helpful, categorized roughly in topics and difficulty. I have added my comments after some entries for reference.

Physics

Electromagnetism

  • Zangwill Andrew (1988). Modern Electrodynamics
  • Jackson J. D. (1999) Classical Electrodynamics (Classic textbook on electrodynamics)
  • Smythe William (1989) Static and Dynamic Electricity (This book is on the difficult side of the spectrum for undergraduate reading, but the author shows off his math skills so much that it’s cool to read.)

Quantum Mechanics

  • Sakurai, J. J.; Napolitano, Jim (2020). Modern Quantum Mechanics
  • Eisberg, Robert; Resnick, Robert (1985). Quantum Physics of Atoms, Molecules, Solids, Nuclei, and Particles 2e (Main textbook used by CKQuantumBros. If you’re interested, you can go to the YouTube channel!)

Quantum Field Theory

  • Lancaster, Tom; Blundell, Stephen, J. (2014). Quantum Field Theory for the Gifted Amateur (This is the textbook used in Professor’s Hsieh’s Quantum Field Theory I course)

Iris Project 2025 - City Algae Dialogues

2025 Iris Project - City Algae Dialogues

This is a self-hosted, small-scale project taking place in Taiwan, aiming to bring a new way for the city to coexist with green residents. City Algae Dialogues explores the overlooked presence and potential of algae within urban ecosystems. Framing algae not as nuisance or decoration, but as a living, responsive collaborator, this project fosters conversations between the city and its microbial residents. Through installations, field research, and speculative storytelling, we reimagine algae as agents of ecological memory, urban renewal, and multispecies dialogue. In a time of environmental crisis and rapid urban change, City Algae Dialogues invites you to listen more closely — to the green voices that grow between cracks, under bridges, and on forgotten walls, and bring them into the hustle of our everyday life.
